# ☑️ LLC Formation Checklist
## Complete Guide to Forming Your Limited Liability Company

---

### BEFORE YOU FILE

- [ ] **Research LLC requirements in your state** — Each state has different fees, forms, and rules. Check your Secretary of State website.
- [ ] **Choose your state of formation** — Most small businesses form in their home state. Delaware/Wyoming/Nevada only make sense for specific situations.
  - ⚠️ Common mistake: Forming in Delaware when you only operate in one state = paying fees in TWO states
- [ ] **Check name availability** — Search your state's business entity database
- [ ] **Ensure name meets state requirements** — Must include "LLC" or "Limited Liability Company." Cannot be too similar to existing entities.
- [ ] **Consider a DBA (Doing Business As)** if you want to operate under a different name
- [ ] **Reserve your business name** (optional, $10-50 in most states, holds name for 60-120 days)

### FORMATION FILING

- [ ] **Choose a registered agent** — Required in all states. Can be yourself (if you have a physical address in the state), a member, or a registered agent service ($50-300/year)
  - ⚠️ Common mistake: Using your home address (becomes public record). Consider a registered agent service for privacy.
- [ ] **Prepare and file Articles of Organization** (called "Certificate of Formation" in some states)
  - Required info: LLC name, registered agent, principal office address, organizer name, management structure
  - Filing fee: $50-500 depending on state
- [ ] **Receive confirmation/filing receipt** from the state (can take 1 day to 4+ weeks depending on state)
- [ ] **File in additional states** if you'll operate in multiple states (foreign LLC registration)

### AFTER FORMATION

- [ ] **Obtain an EIN (Employer Identification Number)** from the IRS — Free, instant online at irs.gov/ein
  - Required for: bank accounts, hiring employees, multi-member LLCs, and most business activities
- [ ] **Draft an Operating Agreement** — Not required in all states but HIGHLY recommended
  - Defines: member ownership %, management structure, profit distribution, voting rights, what happens if a member leaves/dies, dissolution procedures
  - ⚠️ Common mistake: Skipping this, especially for single-member LLCs. Banks may require it, and it strengthens your liability protection.
- [ ] **Open a business bank account** — Keep personal and business finances SEPARATE
  - Bring: EIN letter, Articles of Organization, Operating Agreement, government-issued ID
  - ⚠️ Common mistake: Commingling personal and business funds = "piercing the corporate veil" = losing LLC liability protection
- [ ] **Get a business credit card** (optional but recommended for expense tracking)

### LICENSES & PERMITS

- [ ] **Research federal licenses** — Required for specific industries (alcohol, firearms, agriculture, aviation, broadcasting, etc.)
- [ ] **Apply for state business license** — Required in most states
- [ ] **Apply for local business license/permit** — Check city and county requirements
- [ ] **Obtain industry-specific licenses** — Professional licenses, health permits, building permits, etc.
- [ ] **Get a sales tax permit** if selling taxable goods/services in your state
- [ ] **Apply for a home occupation permit** if operating from home

### TAX SETUP

- [ ] **Choose your tax classification** — Single-member LLC (default: disregarded entity/Schedule C), Multi-member LLC (default: partnership), or elect S-Corp taxation (Form 2553)
  - 💡 Tip: Consult a CPA. S-Corp election can save significant self-employment taxes at higher income levels.
- [ ] **Register for state tax accounts** — Income tax withholding, unemployment insurance, sales tax (as applicable)
- [ ] **Set up quarterly estimated tax payments** if applicable (IRS Form 1040-ES)
- [ ] **Understand self-employment tax obligations** — 15.3% on net earnings (Social Security + Medicare)
- [ ] **Set up a bookkeeping system** — QuickBooks, Wave, Xero, or spreadsheet at minimum

### COMPLIANCE & ONGOING

- [ ] **Understand your annual report requirements** — Most states require an annual or biennial filing ($0-800+)
- [ ] **Know your state's franchise tax** if applicable (e.g., California $800/year minimum, Texas margin tax, etc.)
- [ ] **Calendar all recurring deadlines** — Annual reports, tax filings, license renewals
- [ ] **Maintain your LLC formalities** — Keep records of major decisions, don't commingle funds, sign contracts as the LLC (not personally)
- [ ] **Get business insurance** — See our Business Insurance Checklist (#7)
- [ ] **Understand your record-keeping obligations** — See our Bookkeeping & Financial Records Checklist (#6)

### OPTIONAL BUT RECOMMENDED

- [ ] **Trademark your business name/logo** — Federal trademark via USPTO ($250-350 per class)
- [ ] **Register your domain name** — Secure .com and relevant extensions
- [ ] **Set up a business email** — Use your domain (name@yourbusiness.com), not Gmail
- [ ] **Create a basic website** — Even a one-page site establishes online presence
- [ ] **Get a business phone number** — Google Voice (free) or dedicated business line
