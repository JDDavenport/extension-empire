# ☑️ State Tax Obligations Guide

---

### STATE INCOME TAX
- [ ] Determine if your state has a state income tax (no income tax: AK, FL, NV, NH*, SD, TN*, TX, WA, WY — *limited)
- [ ] Register for state income tax withholding (if employees)
- [ ] Understand pass-through entity tax elections (many states now offer PTET to circumvent SALT cap)
- [ ] File state income tax return (deadlines usually follow federal)
- [ ] Make state estimated tax payments (usually same dates as federal)

### SALES TAX
- [ ] Determine if you sell taxable goods or services in your state
- [ ] Register for a sales tax permit / seller's permit
- [ ] Understand your tax rate (state + county + city + special districts)
- [ ] Know your filing frequency (monthly, quarterly, or annual based on volume)
- [ ] Collect sales tax at point of sale
- [ ] File sales tax returns and remit collected tax on time
- [ ] Understand marketplace facilitator laws (Amazon, Etsy collect on your behalf in most states)

### SALES TAX NEXUS (Multi-State Sellers)
- [ ] Understand economic nexus thresholds (typically $100K revenue OR 200 transactions in a state)
- [ ] Track sales by state to identify nexus triggers
- [ ] Register for sales tax in states where you have nexus
- [ ] Consider sales tax automation software (TaxJar, Avalara) if selling in multiple states

### FRANCHISE / GROSS RECEIPTS TAX
- [ ] Check if your state has a franchise tax, gross receipts tax, or privilege tax:
  - California: $800/year minimum franchise tax
  - Texas: Margin tax (if revenue > $1.23M)
  - Delaware: Annual franchise tax ($300-200K+ depending on shares/assets)
  - Ohio: Commercial Activity Tax
  - Washington: Business & Occupation Tax
  - Nevada: Commerce Tax (if revenue > $4M)
- [ ] File and pay franchise/gross receipts tax on time

### UNEMPLOYMENT INSURANCE
- [ ] Register for state unemployment insurance (SUI)
- [ ] Understand your state's UI tax rate (new employer rate varies)
- [ ] File quarterly UI reports
- [ ] Pay UI taxes on time

### OTHER STATE TAXES
- [ ] Business personal property tax (many states tax business equipment, furniture, etc.)
- [ ] Use tax (for purchases where sales tax wasn't collected)
- [ ] Excise taxes (specific industries: fuel, tobacco, alcohol)
- [ ] Annual report / business registration renewal (not a tax but required)
