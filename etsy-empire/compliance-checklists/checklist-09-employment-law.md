# ☑️ Employment Law Compliance Checklist

---

### HIRING PROCESS
- [ ] Job descriptions written (essential functions, qualifications, physical requirements)
- [ ] Non-discriminatory hiring practices (no prohibited interview questions: age, marital status, religion, disability, pregnancy, national origin)
- [ ] Background check consent obtained (if conducting background checks)
- [ ] Form I-9 completed for EVERY new hire within 3 days of start date
- [ ] W-4 completed by every employee
- [ ] State new hire reporting filed (required in all states, typically within 20 days)
- [ ] Offer letter or employment agreement provided
- [ ] At-will employment disclaimer included (if applicable in your state)
- [ ] E-Verify used (required for federal contractors; optional but available for all employers)

### WAGE & HOUR COMPLIANCE
- [ ] Federal minimum wage met ($7.25/hr — many states/cities are higher, check yours)
- [ ] State/local minimum wage met (check YOUR state and city)
- [ ] Employees properly classified: Exempt vs. Non-exempt (FLSA)
  - ⚠️ Common mistake: Misclassifying employees as exempt to avoid overtime
- [ ] Overtime paid correctly (1.5x after 40 hrs/week for non-exempt; some states have daily OT)
- [ ] Contractors vs. employees properly classified (IRS 20-factor test / ABC test)
  - ⚠️ Misclassification penalties can be severe: back taxes, penalties, benefits owed
- [ ] Accurate time records maintained for all non-exempt employees
- [ ] Pay stubs provided with required information (varies by state)
- [ ] Equal pay for equal work (Equal Pay Act compliance)
- [ ] Tip credit rules followed (if applicable)
- [ ] Meal and rest break requirements met (state-specific)

### REQUIRED POSTINGS
- [ ] Federal postings displayed (FLSA, OSHA, EEO, FMLA, USERRA, EPPA)
  - Free from dol.gov/agencies/whd/posters
- [ ] State-required postings displayed (varies — check your state DOL)
- [ ] Postings accessible to all employees (including remote — electronic posting may suffice)

### ANTI-DISCRIMINATION
- [ ] EEO policy established and communicated
- [ ] Anti-harassment policy with reporting procedure
- [ ] Harassment training conducted (required in CA, CT, DE, IL, ME, NY and others)
- [ ] Reasonable accommodation process for disabilities (ADA — 15+ employees)
- [ ] Religious accommodation process
- [ ] Pregnancy accommodation (PWFA — all employers with 15+ employees)

### LEAVE REQUIREMENTS
- [ ] FMLA compliance (50+ employees): 12 weeks unpaid leave for qualifying reasons
- [ ] State family/medical leave laws (many states have expanded or paid leave)
- [ ] Jury duty leave (required in most states)
- [ ] Military leave (USERRA — all employers)
- [ ] Voting leave (state-specific)
- [ ] Sick leave (many states/cities require paid sick leave)

### TERMINATION
- [ ] Termination documented with reason
- [ ] Final paycheck timing requirements met (varies by state — some require same day)
- [ ] COBRA notice provided (if 20+ employees)
- [ ] State mini-COBRA notice (smaller employers)
- [ ] Unemployment insurance information provided
- [ ] Company property returned
- [ ] System access revoked
- [ ] Exit interview conducted (optional but recommended)
- [ ] Personnel file maintained per retention requirements

### RECORD KEEPING
- [ ] Personnel files maintained securely
- [ ] I-9 forms stored separately from personnel files
- [ ] Medical records stored separately (ADA requirement)
- [ ] Payroll records retained minimum 3 years (7 recommended)
- [ ] I-9 forms retained: 3 years from hire date OR 1 year after termination (whichever is later)

---

**⚠️ Employment law is complex and varies significantly by state and employer size. Consult an employment attorney.**
