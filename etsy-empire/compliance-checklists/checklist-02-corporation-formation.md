# ☑️ Corporation Formation Checklist
## C-Corp and S-Corp Formation Guide

---

### PRE-FORMATION

- [ ] **Decide: C-Corp vs S-Corp** — C-Corp: double taxation but unlimited shareholders, multiple stock classes. S-Corp: pass-through taxation but limited to 100 shareholders, one stock class, US citizens/residents only.
- [ ] **Check name availability** in your state of incorporation
- [ ] **Reserve corporate name** if needed ($10-50, optional)
- [ ] **Choose state of incorporation** — Home state for most small corps; Delaware for venture-backed startups
- [ ] **Select a registered agent** in your state of incorporation

### FORMATION FILING

- [ ] **Draft and file Articles of Incorporation** (Certificate of Incorporation in some states)
  - Info needed: Corporate name, registered agent, authorized shares (number and par value), incorporator name, purpose clause
  - Filing fee: $50-500+ depending on state and authorized shares
- [ ] **Receive filed articles/certificate** from Secretary of State
- [ ] **Obtain EIN** from IRS (irs.gov/ein — free, instant)

### CORPORATE ORGANIZATION

- [ ] **Draft corporate bylaws** — Rules governing the corporation: meetings, voting, officer roles, stock issuance, amendment procedures
- [ ] **Hold organizational meeting of incorporator(s)**
  - Adopt bylaws
  - Elect initial board of directors
  - Appoint officers (President, Secretary, Treasurer at minimum)
  - Authorize stock issuance
  - Adopt fiscal year
  - Approve registered agent
  - Approve banking resolutions
- [ ] **Document organizational meeting minutes** and file in corporate records book
- [ ] **Issue stock certificates** to initial shareholders
  - Record in stock ledger: shareholder name, shares, date, consideration paid
  - 83(b) election within 30 days if shares subject to vesting (CRITICAL for founders)
- [ ] **Execute shareholder agreement** (if multiple shareholders)
  - Covers: voting rights, transfer restrictions, buy-sell provisions, drag-along/tag-along rights
- [ ] **Set up corporate records book** (physical or digital)
  - Articles of Incorporation, Bylaws, Meeting Minutes, Stock Ledger, Resolutions

### S-CORP ELECTION (if applicable)

- [ ] **File IRS Form 2553** — Must be filed within 75 days of incorporation (or by March 15 for calendar year)
  - ⚠️ Common mistake: Missing the 75-day window. Late elections require reasonable cause letter.
- [ ] **All shareholders sign Form 2553**
- [ ] **Verify state S-Corp recognition** — Most states follow federal election, but some (e.g., NYC) require separate filing
- [ ] **Set up reasonable salary** for shareholder-employees (required by IRS)

### TAX & FINANCIAL SETUP

- [ ] **Open business bank account** — Bring: EIN letter, Articles, Bylaws, Banking Resolution, Officer IDs
- [ ] **Set up payroll** if you have employees (including shareholder-employees of S-Corps)
- [ ] **Register for state tax accounts** — Withholding, unemployment, sales tax
- [ ] **Set up accounting system** — Corporations have stricter accounting requirements than LLCs
- [ ] **Understand estimated tax payment obligations**
- [ ] **Know your annual tax return deadlines** — C-Corp: Form 1120 (April 15), S-Corp: Form 1120-S (March 15)

### LICENSES & COMPLIANCE

- [ ] **Obtain all required business licenses and permits** (see Checklist #3)
- [ ] **Get business insurance** (see Checklist #7)
- [ ] **Understand annual report requirements** for your state
- [ ] **Know your franchise tax obligations** (varies by state)
- [ ] **Hold annual shareholder meeting** (required)
- [ ] **Hold annual board of directors meeting** (required)
- [ ] **Document all major decisions** with written resolutions or meeting minutes

### ONGOING CORPORATE FORMALITIES

- [ ] **Maintain separate corporate finances** — NEVER commingle
- [ ] **Sign contracts as the corporation** — "Name, Title, on behalf of [Corp Name]"
- [ ] **File annual reports** with Secretary of State
- [ ] **Hold required annual meetings** and document minutes
- [ ] **Keep corporate records book updated**
- [ ] **File all tax returns on time**
- [ ] **Renew licenses and permits annually**
