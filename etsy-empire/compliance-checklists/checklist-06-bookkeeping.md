# ☑️ Bookkeeping & Financial Records Checklist

---

### SYSTEM SETUP
- [ ] Choose accounting software (QuickBooks, Xero, Wave, FreshBooks) or spreadsheet system
- [ ] Set up chart of accounts appropriate for your business type
- [ ] Connect business bank account and credit card for automatic transaction import
- [ ] Choose accounting method: Cash or Accrual (be consistent)
- [ ] Set up invoicing system for billing clients

### DAILY/WEEKLY TRACKING
- [ ] Record all income/revenue as received
- [ ] Record all expenses and categorize properly
- [ ] Save all receipts (digital photo/scan system recommended: Dext, Hubdoc, or phone camera)
- [ ] Reconcile point-of-sale / payment processor transactions
- [ ] Track cash transactions separately

### MONTHLY TASKS
- [ ] Reconcile bank statements with accounting records
- [ ] Reconcile credit card statements
- [ ] Review accounts receivable — follow up on unpaid invoices
- [ ] Review accounts payable — pay bills on time
- [ ] Categorize any uncategorized transactions
- [ ] Generate and review Profit & Loss statement
- [ ] Generate and review Balance Sheet
- [ ] Review cash flow — ensure upcoming obligations can be met
- [ ] Set aside money for estimated tax payments (25-30% of profit recommended)

### QUARTERLY TASKS
- [ ] Review P&L vs. budget/goals
- [ ] Prepare and file quarterly tax payments (estimated + payroll)
- [ ] Review and update financial projections
- [ ] Meet with CPA/bookkeeper if applicable

### ANNUAL TASKS
- [ ] Year-end close: ensure all transactions recorded and categorized
- [ ] Generate annual financial statements (P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow)
- [ ] Reconcile all accounts
- [ ] Prepare 1099s for contractors ($600+ threshold)
- [ ] Prepare W-2s for employees
- [ ] Gather documents for tax preparation
- [ ] File annual tax returns (or provide records to CPA)
- [ ] Review and update budget for next year

### RECORD RETENTION (How Long to Keep)
| Record Type | Retention Period |
|------------|----------------|
| Tax returns | Permanently |
| Supporting tax documents | 7 years |
| Bank statements | 7 years |
| Receipts and invoices | 7 years |
| Payroll records | 7 years |
| Business formation docs | Permanently |
| Contracts and agreements | 7 years after expiration |
| Insurance policies | Permanently |
| Asset/depreciation records | 7 years after disposal |
| Employee records | 7 years after termination |

---

**⚠️ Consult your CPA for record retention requirements specific to your industry and state.**
