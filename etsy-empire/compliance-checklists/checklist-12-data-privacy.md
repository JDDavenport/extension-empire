# ☑️ Data Privacy Compliance Checklist

---

### GENERAL DATA PRIVACY
- [ ] **Identify what personal data you collect** (names, emails, addresses, payment info, browsing data, cookies)
- [ ] **Document your data processing activities** — What data, why, how long, who has access
- [ ] **Privacy policy published** on your website — Must be clear, accurate, and accessible
  - What you collect, why, how you use it, who you share it with, how to opt out
- [ ] **Consent mechanisms in place** — Opt-in for marketing emails, cookie consent, data collection notices
- [ ] **Data minimization** — Only collect data you actually need
- [ ] **Data retention policy** — Define how long you keep data, when/how you delete it
- [ ] **Data security measures** — Encryption, access controls, secure storage
- [ ] **Vendor/third-party data sharing agreements** — Know where your data flows (analytics, email providers, payment processors)

### CAN-SPAM ACT (Email Marketing)
- [ ] Don't use false/misleading header information
- [ ] Don't use deceptive subject lines
- [ ] Identify the message as an ad (if applicable)
- [ ] Include your physical mailing address
- [ ] Include clear unsubscribe mechanism
- [ ] Honor opt-out requests within 10 business days
- [ ] Monitor what third parties do on your behalf

### CCPA/CPRA (California — If Applicable)
Applies if: Annual revenue >$25M, OR data of 100K+ consumers, OR 50%+ revenue from selling data
- [ ] "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link on website
- [ ] Right to know what data is collected
- [ ] Right to delete personal data
- [ ] Right to opt out of sale/sharing
- [ ] Right to non-discrimination for exercising rights
- [ ] Privacy policy updated for CCPA requirements
- [ ] Data processing agreements with service providers

### GDPR (If Serving EU/UK Customers)
- [ ] Lawful basis for processing identified (consent, contract, legitimate interest, etc.)
- [ ] Cookie consent banner with granular options (not just "accept all")
- [ ] Right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, port data
- [ ] Data Protection Impact Assessment for high-risk processing
- [ ] Data breach notification process (72 hours to supervisory authority)
- [ ] Representative in EU designated (if no EU establishment)

### DATA BREACH PREPAREDNESS
- [ ] **Incident response plan** documented
- [ ] Know your state's breach notification requirements (all 50 states have them)
- [ ] Notification templates prepared (for customers and regulators)
- [ ] Breach notification timeline understood (varies: 30-72 hours to 60 days by jurisdiction)
- [ ] Cyber insurance in place (see Insurance Checklist #7)
- [ ] Regular security assessments conducted

### PAYMENT DATA (PCI-DSS)
- [ ] If processing credit cards: PCI-DSS compliance level determined
- [ ] Use a PCI-compliant payment processor (Stripe, Square, PayPal handle most requirements)
- [ ] Never store full credit card numbers, CVVs, or PINs
- [ ] Secure transmission of payment data (HTTPS/TLS)
- [ ] Annual PCI self-assessment questionnaire completed (if applicable)

---

**⚠️ Data privacy law is rapidly evolving. Many states are passing new laws. Consult a privacy attorney.**
