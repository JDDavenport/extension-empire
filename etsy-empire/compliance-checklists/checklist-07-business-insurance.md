# ☑️ Business Insurance Checklist

---

### ESSENTIAL COVERAGE (Most Businesses Need)

- [ ] **General Liability Insurance** — Covers third-party bodily injury, property damage, and advertising injury. Essential for ALL businesses.
  - Typical cost: $400-1,500/year for small businesses
  - Common limits: $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate

- [ ] **Professional Liability (E&O) Insurance** — Covers claims of negligence, mistakes, or failure to deliver services. Essential for service businesses, consultants, freelancers.
  - Who needs it: Consultants, IT professionals, accountants, real estate agents, designers, marketers

- [ ] **Commercial Property Insurance** — Covers your business property: equipment, inventory, furniture, fixtures. Needed if you have a physical location or valuable equipment.
  - Covers: Fire, theft, vandalism, certain natural disasters
  - Note: Flood and earthquake typically require separate policies

- [ ] **Business Owner's Policy (BOP)** — Bundles general liability + commercial property (often cheaper than buying separately). Great for small businesses with a physical location.

### EMPLOYEE-RELATED COVERAGE

- [ ] **Workers' Compensation Insurance** — REQUIRED in almost all states if you have employees (even one). Covers medical expenses and lost wages for work-related injuries.
  - ⚠️ Penalties for not having workers' comp can be severe (fines + personal liability)
  - Check your state's requirements — some exempt very small businesses or certain industries

- [ ] **Employment Practices Liability (EPLI)** — Covers claims of discrimination, wrongful termination, harassment, retaliation by employees. Important once you have 5+ employees.

- [ ] **Group Health Insurance** — Required for businesses with 50+ full-time employees (ACA). Optional but competitive benefit for smaller businesses.
  - Small Business Health Options Program (SHOP) for businesses with 1-50 employees

### SPECIALIZED COVERAGE

- [ ] **Cyber Liability Insurance** — Covers data breaches, ransomware, customer data exposure, business interruption from cyber events. Essential if you handle customer data, process payments, or operate online.
  - ⚠️ Growing risk — even small businesses are targets

- [ ] **Commercial Auto Insurance** — Required if your business owns vehicles or employees drive for business purposes.
  - Personal auto policies typically exclude business use
  - Hired & Non-Owned Auto covers employees using personal vehicles for business

- [ ] **Product Liability Insurance** — If you manufacture, distribute, or sell physical products. Covers injury/damage caused by your products.

- [ ] **Business Interruption Insurance** — Covers lost income if you can't operate due to a covered event (fire, natural disaster, etc.). Usually added to a BOP or property policy.

- [ ] **Key Person Insurance** — Life/disability insurance on critical people in your business. If they're gone, the business gets a payout to survive the transition.

- [ ] **Directors & Officers (D&O) Insurance** — Covers personal liability of corporate directors and officers. Important for corporations with boards.

- [ ] **Umbrella Policy** — Additional liability coverage above your other policy limits. Relatively cheap for the extra protection.

### INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC

- [ ] **Liquor Liability** — If you serve or sell alcohol
- [ ] **Inland Marine** — Equipment that moves location to location (contractors, photographers)
- [ ] **Builder's Risk** — Construction projects
- [ ] **Professional Bonding** — Required for some licensed professionals
- [ ] **Commercial Crime** — Employee theft, forgery, fraud

### GETTING INSURED

- [ ] Get quotes from at least 3 providers
- [ ] Work with an independent insurance broker (shops multiple carriers for you)
- [ ] Review policy exclusions carefully
- [ ] Understand deductibles and how they affect premiums
- [ ] Ask about bundle discounts (BOP, multiple policies)
- [ ] Review certificates of insurance requirements from clients/landlords
- [ ] Set calendar reminders for annual policy reviews

### ANNUAL REVIEW
- [ ] Review all coverage amounts — still adequate?
- [ ] Update property values and inventory amounts
- [ ] Report any changes: new locations, employees, revenue, services
- [ ] Shop for competitive rates (every 2-3 years)
- [ ] Review claims history and adjust if needed
