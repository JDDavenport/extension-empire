# ☑️ Federal Tax Obligations Checklist

---

### INITIAL SETUP
- [ ] **Obtain EIN** from IRS (irs.gov/ein)
- [ ] **Determine tax classification** — Sole prop (Schedule C), Partnership (1065), S-Corp (1120-S), C-Corp (1120)
- [ ] **Choose fiscal year** — Calendar year (Jan-Dec) or fiscal year (requires IRS approval for some entities)
- [ ] **Choose accounting method** — Cash basis or accrual basis
- [ ] **Set up a system to track income and expenses** from day one

### QUARTERLY OBLIGATIONS
- [ ] **Estimated tax payments** (Form 1040-ES) — Due: April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15
  - Required if you expect to owe $1,000+ in taxes
  - ⚠️ Penalty for underpayment — aim for 100% of prior year tax or 90% of current year
- [ ] **Payroll tax deposits** (if you have employees)
  - Federal income tax withholding + employee FICA
  - Employer FICA match (6.2% SS + 1.45% Medicare)
  - Deposit schedule: semi-weekly or monthly based on payroll size
  - File Form 941 quarterly
- [ ] **FUTA tax** (Federal Unemployment) — 6% on first $7,000 per employee (credit for state unemployment = usually 0.6% net)

### ANNUAL FILINGS
- [ ] **Income tax return** — Due dates vary by entity type:
  - Sole Prop/Single-member LLC: Schedule C with Form 1040 (April 15)
  - Partnership/Multi-member LLC: Form 1065 + K-1s (March 15)
  - S-Corp: Form 1120-S + K-1s (March 15)
  - C-Corp: Form 1120 (April 15)
- [ ] **Self-employment tax** — Schedule SE (15.3% on net self-employment income)
- [ ] **Issue 1099-NEC forms** to contractors paid $600+ (due January 31)
- [ ] **Issue W-2 forms** to employees (due January 31)
- [ ] **File Form W-3** (transmittal of W-2s) with SSA (due January 31)
- [ ] **File Form 940** (Annual FUTA return) — due January 31
- [ ] **File information returns** — 1099-INT, 1099-DIV, 1099-MISC as applicable

### RECORD KEEPING
- [ ] Keep **all receipts and records for 3-7 years** (7 years recommended)
- [ ] Maintain records of: income, expenses, assets, depreciation, vehicle use, home office, travel, meals
- [ ] Keep copies of all filed tax returns permanently
- [ ] Document basis in business assets for depreciation and sale calculations

### DEDUCTIONS TO TRACK
- [ ] Home office expenses (simplified or actual method)
- [ ] Vehicle/mileage (standard rate or actual expenses)
- [ ] Business travel, meals (50% deductible)
- [ ] Health insurance premiums (self-employed deduction)
- [ ] Retirement plan contributions (SEP-IRA, Solo 401k, SIMPLE)
- [ ] Equipment and supplies (Section 179 / bonus depreciation)
- [ ] Professional services (accounting, legal, consulting)
- [ ] Software and subscriptions
- [ ] Marketing and advertising
- [ ] Business insurance premiums
- [ ] Education and professional development
