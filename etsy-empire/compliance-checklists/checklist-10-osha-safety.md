# ☑️ Workplace Safety (OSHA) Compliance Checklist

---

### GENERAL REQUIREMENTS (All Employers)
- [ ] **OSHA poster displayed** ("Job Safety and Health: It's The Law") in conspicuous location
  - Free at osha.gov/publications/poster
- [ ] **General Duty Clause** understood — Provide a workplace free from recognized hazards
- [ ] **OSHA 300 Log** maintained (if 10+ employees) — Record all work-related injuries/illnesses
- [ ] **OSHA 300A Summary** posted Feb 1 - April 30 each year
- [ ] **OSHA 301 Incident Reports** completed within 7 days of incident
- [ ] **Severe injuries reported to OSHA** — Fatality: 8 hours. Hospitalization/amputation/eye loss: 24 hours.

### SAFETY PROGRAM
- [ ] Written safety and health program established
- [ ] Safety responsibilities assigned (safety officer/committee)
- [ ] Regular workplace safety inspections conducted
- [ ] Hazard identification and correction process in place
- [ ] Near-miss reporting encouraged and tracked
- [ ] Safety suggestions from employees welcomed and addressed

### EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS
- [ ] Emergency Action Plan (EAP) written and communicated (required if 10+ employees)
- [ ] Emergency exits clearly marked and unobstructed
- [ ] Evacuation routes posted
- [ ] Fire extinguishers available, inspected monthly, serviced annually
- [ ] First aid kit stocked and accessible
- [ ] Emergency contact numbers posted
- [ ] Emergency drills conducted (at least annually)
- [ ] Severe weather procedures established

### TRAINING
- [ ] New employee safety orientation completed before starting work
- [ ] Hazard Communication training (right to know about chemical hazards)
- [ ] Fire extinguisher training
- [ ] Emergency procedure training
- [ ] Job-specific safety training documented
- [ ] Refresher training conducted annually or when hazards change
- [ ] Training records maintained

### HAZARD COMMUNICATION (HazCom)
- [ ] Written HazCom program
- [ ] Safety Data Sheets (SDS) available for ALL hazardous chemicals
- [ ] Chemical containers properly labeled (GHS format)
- [ ] Employees trained on HazCom and SDS access
- [ ] Chemical inventory list maintained

### PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)
- [ ] Hazard assessment conducted to determine PPE needs
- [ ] PPE provided at no cost to employees
- [ ] PPE training provided (proper use, maintenance, limitations)
- [ ] PPE inspection and replacement schedule

### SPECIFIC HAZARDS (Check if Applicable)
- [ ] Electrical safety — Proper wiring, GFCI, lockout/tagout
- [ ] Fall protection — Guardrails, harnesses for work at heights
- [ ] Machine guarding — Moving parts properly guarded
- [ ] Confined space entry procedures
- [ ] Forklift/powered industrial truck training and certification
- [ ] Bloodborne pathogens program (healthcare, first responders)
- [ ] Respiratory protection program
- [ ] Hearing conservation program (noise > 85 dB)
- [ ] Ergonomics assessment and controls

---

**⚠️ OSHA requirements vary by industry and hazard exposure. Visit osha.gov for industry-specific standards.**
